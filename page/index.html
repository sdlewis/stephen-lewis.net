Local Set Approximation

Local set approximation (LSA) is the study of the local structure of sets through their tangents and pseudotangents. This substantially generalizes smooth surfaces, which are a sets with continuously varying tangent planes. LSA is a natural framework for studying the geometry of sets with singularities. For example, we give sufficient conditions on the tangents and psuedotangents of a set $A$ to conclude that $A$ decomposes into a regular and a singular part.

• Joint w/ Matt Badger. Local set approximation: Mattila-Vuorinen type sets, Reifenberg type sets, and tangent sets. Submitted. arXiv, pdf

• Local set approximation: infinitesimal to local theorems and applications. Doctoroal Thesis (2012), University of Washington. pdf

Geometric Measure Theory

Given a measure in Euclidean space, what is the connection between the quantitative properties of the measure and its geometric structure? This research explores this question through a novel aspect of singular set geometry.

• Singular points of Hölder asymptotically optimally doubling measures. Submitted. arXiv

Algebra

The full combinatorics arising from matrix representations of $U_n(\mathbb{F}_q)$ is computationally impossible. However a smaller class of the respresentations called supercharacters can be used functionally to solve problems (such as random walks) which we here analyze.

• Joint w/ the following. Supercharacters, symmetric functions in noncommuting variables, and related Hopf algebras. Advances in Math 229 (2012), no. 4, 2310-2337. arXiv

• Joint w/ Nat Thiem. Nonzero coefficients in restriction and tensor products of supercharacters of $U_n(\mathbb{F}_q)$. Advances in Math 227 (2011), 40-72. arXiv

• Restriction of supercharacters of $U_n(\mathbb{F}_2)$. Undergraduate Honors Thesis (2009) at the University of Colorado, Boulder. pdf
